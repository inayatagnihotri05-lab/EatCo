Case Study — Behaviour Change in Action (EBAP)

Project: Eco-Behaviour Acceleration Project (EBAP) — a longitudinal field study (2023–2024) involving 1,200 students across 18 schools. The project compared three approaches: traditional lectures (control), regular sustainability tasks, and a fully gamified programme with digital tracking (EatCo-like).

“Interactive, measurable interventions dramatically outperform passive instruction — supporting the theory that engagement + feedback = behaviour adoption.”

Below we summarise the most important, statistically significant outcomes from the study.

72% Adoption rate of new sustainable habits (Group C)
480 g Average weekly waste reduction per student (Group C)
41% Increase in plant-forward meal choices (Group C)
81% Knowledge retention after 60 days (Group C)

Detailed findings

  • Behaviour adoption: Gamified participants (Group C) showed a 72% adoption rate versus 11% in the control group — indicating a clear causal effect of design and feedback loops.
  • Waste impact: Aggregated across the study cohort, Group C produced ~576 kg less waste per week (1,200 students scaled down by group proportions), demonstrating measurable local impact.
  • Carbon implications: The observed dietary shift corresponds to an estimated reduction of 0.8–1.2 tonnes CO₂ per student annually for highly engaged participants who maintained plant-forward choices — depending on baseline diets.
  • Motivation: 87% of students reported that sustainability felt “enjoyable” after gamified engagement; 73% stated they would continue behaviours beyond the study period.

How EatCo replicates EBAP

  1. Micro-learning: Short, science-backed lessons (5–10 minutes).
  2. Feedback loops: Progress tracking, streaks, and badges.
  3. Social proof: Leaderboards and community challenges.
  4. Real-world prompts: Photo logging, waste metrics, and simple data visualisations.

Why This Matters

Environmental change is a systems problem, but systems are made of people. By focusing on measurable individual behaviours, EatCo leverages cumulative impact: small, sustained changes at scale create meaningful reductions in waste and emissions. Educational design grounded in evidence — coupled with digital engagement — accelerates adoption and retention.
